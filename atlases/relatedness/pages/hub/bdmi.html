Bateson–Dobzhansky–Muller incompatibility (BDMI) screen. For each inversion candidate, runs six population-genetic tests that flag candidate incompatibility regions: Mendelian segregation distortion, missing karyotype classes, heterozygote excess / deficit, ancestry × genotype interaction, long-range forbidden haplotype combinations, and (when phenotype data exist) phenotype association. Results combine into a per-candidate confidence ladder (weak → moderate → strong → very strong). WGS-only signals are correlative — controlled crosses are required for validation. MANUSCRIPT PHRASING
Although direct validation of Bateson–Dobzhansky–Muller incompatibilities requires controlled crosses or fitness measurements, the present dataset allows candidate incompatibility regions to be screened by testing for segregation distortion, depletion of expected inversion genotype classes, ancestry-dependent karyotype frequencies, and long-range non-random associations between inversion arrangements and genomic backgrounds.
