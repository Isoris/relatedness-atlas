Meiotic crossover observables. From offspring genotypes alone you can estimate single-CO and double-CO frequencies and derive the coefficient of coincidence C and the interference I = 1 − C. The latent two-step model (precondition d followed by exchange x) is not identifiable from genotype data because the three observed quantities (r₁, r₂, r₁₂) underdetermine the four unknowns (a, b, d, x). Treat d and x as conceptual placeholders only — separate them only if you have an independent assay (DSBs, recombination nodules, SPO11 oligos, MLH1 foci).

Model: r₁ = x(a + d), r₂ = x(b + d), r₁₂ = x²·d. Three equations, four unknowns ⇒ d and x are not separable from genotype counts alone. Reporting C and I is the publishable thing; reporting d or x requires an independent assay.
WHAT "PRECONDITION OF EXCHANGE" MEANS
The precondition layer (a / b / d in the model) is not one molecule. It's the whole upstream meiotic setup that makes a region competent for crossing over. In modern words it includes: homolog pairing, synapsis / synaptonemal complex formation, chromatin accessibility, double-strand-break formation, recombination nodules, strand-invasion intermediates, crossover designation, and interference signalling along the chromosome. The exchange layer (x) is only the final resolution step — the cut-and-join that turns a designated site into a real chiasma.
  • Predisposition layer = where crossovers are allowed / designated. Traced by NCO rate (Eligibility tab).
  • Resolution layer = which designated events become actual crossovers. Traced by CO/(NCO+CO) (Resolution tab).
The point of C in this framework: C asks whether two intervals become crossover-competent together more or less often than expected — not just whether the final exchange physically happened.

E. Why inversions suppress observable CO (geometry, not nucleotides)
SEQUENCE-PRESERVING, GEOMETRY-ALTERING

Inversions preserve most local nucleotide content, so they may not necessarily alter intrinsic DSB motif potential; however, in heterokaryotypes they alter homologous pairing geometry, which can reduce crossover designation / resolution and the recovery of crossover products while still allowing NCO / gene-conversion tracts.

Normal homolog:  A B C D E F G H
Inverted homolog: A B F E D C G H

Same nucleotides, different orientation — so to align homologous bases the chromosome must form an inversion loop. The loop changes synapsis, strand-invasion geometry, crossover designation, crossover resolution, and the recoverability of recombinant chromatids. DSBs and gene conversion can still occur inside the inversion; what disappears from the observable count is the resolved crossover product.

Mapped onto the latent two-step model from Section B:

  • d (precondition) — partly preserved when sequence-driven DSB competence is unchanged. But the inversion loop can still disrupt synapsis / homolog engagement, so d is not fully spared.
  • x (exchange / resolution) — typically the larger change. Crossover products that arise inside the loop are unbalanced and lost from the observable offspring set; NCO / gene-conversion tracts can still come through.
