For linked inversions on the same chromosome, apparent distortion at one candidate should be tested conditionally on the other candidate or on the inferred long-range haplotype regime. If the distortion disappears after conditioning, the signal is better interpreted as haplotype-regime coupling than as independent underdominance, meiotic drive, or BDMI. Once a residual signal survives the regime control, the cross-type classifier separates meiotic drive (the parent transmits one arrangement too often) from underdominance (the heterozygote offspring class is depleted regardless of cross).

C. Meiotic drive vs underdominance (cross-type classifier)
Aggregates DEMO.triads by parent cross-type at the focal candidate. Drive candidate = AB parent under-transmits the same allele across crosses (AB depleted in AA×AB but AB elevated in AB×BB when A is over-transmitted). Underdominance candidate = the AB offspring class is depleted regardless of cross direction (AA×AB, AB×BB, AB×AB all show AB deficit). The classifier defers to Section B when the focal is regime-explained.

INTERPRETATION HIERARCHY
Before calling a candidate underdominance, meiotic drive, or BDMI, check these in order:
  1. Single-inversion distortion (Section A — focal HWE marginal).
  2. Conditional on nearby inversion (Section B — partner table).
  3. Conditional on same-chromosome haplotype regime (Section B — coupled / explained / residual verdict).
  4. Conditional on genome-wide ancestry (cross-page — BDMI Test D).
  5. Conditional on family / pedigree (cross-page — Mendelian tab cohort dyads / triads).
  6. Only then assign a mechanistic label (Section C — drive vs underdominance), and only after that go to BDMI / incompatibility (BDMI tab).
